bio

inicio / home

Región Carbonífera de Coahuila, México, 1985.

español

Artista visual y sonoro, videoasta y promotor cultural independiente. Ha colaborado con escritores, cineastas, artistas visuales, teatristas, bailarines, artistas conceptuales, entre otros. Fue becario del PECDA Coahuila en 2015 en la categoría de jóvenes creadores en el área de artes digitales.
Su obra reflexiona acerca del papel de la memoria y la nostalgia en la creación artística, el uso de nuevas tecnologías, la materialidad de los espacios cibernéticos, y se cuestiona los límites que definen a la figura autoral.

english

Sound artist, videomaker, and independent cultural promoter. He has colaborated with writers, filmmakers, visual artists, dramatists, dancers, conceptual artists, among others. He earned a PECDA Coahuila fellowship in 2015 in the "jóvenes creadores" category in the digital arts area.
His work ponders on the role of memory and nostalgia in artistic creation, the use of new technologies, the materiality of cybernetic spaces, and questions the limits that define the authoral figure.

This section was inspired by The Setup. It's only available in English and it's not in my plans to translate it into Spanish.

Who are you and what do you do?

I am Jorge Rangel and I’m a musician, visual artist, multimedia artist and writer. I have collaborated with writers, visual artists, musicians, actors, performance artists, filmmakers, etc. I also coordinate artistic workshops, mostly focused on sound production and multimedia / new media art.

What hardware do you use?

I use a MacBook Pro 13” 2012 non-Retina which I got because it’s so much more upgradeable than any other currently available model. I have an iPod touch 5 which I use for all the apps everybody uses on their iPhone. I don’t have a smartphone, I use a Nokia X3 when I remember to charge its battery. I’ve successfully managed to live without depending on a mobile phone for a few years now.
For music and sound production I use a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 interface and the Novation Launchpad Mini which I love. When I play live (and I seldom do) I use a Line 6 FBV Express MkII foot MIDI controller. I also have a cheap Silvertone Strat-clone-gone-wrong which is enough for me.
For drawing I use mostly sharpies and a variety of stylographs and pencils, usually on Moleskines, sketchpads or index cards.
I read on a Kindle.

And what software?

For music and sound I use Ableton Live 9, mainly. Sometimes I also use SunVox, GarageBand, Reaper and Max. I’m trying to learn Supercollider.
For visual and multimedia art I use a variety of tools: Processing for generative visuals and image glitching; Max for processing images and video using Vizzie; Affinity Photo for image editing; Affinity Designer for logos and other illustratons; Final Cut Pro and iMovie for video editing, depending on the complexity of the task at hand.
I write HTML in Atom and upload it to GitHub. I use the default apps that come with a Mac: I write on Pages and sometimes Text Edit, check my email accounts on Mail, get organized with Calendar and Reminders. I sync everything with iCloud. The integration with Mac OS X and iOS makes using these apps a no-brainer for me.
I keep a journal on Day One, which I love.
Sometimes I make small programs in PICO-8.
I game mostly on Steam but also enjoy NES and SNES games on OpenEMU and DOS games on DOSBox.

What would be your dream setup?

I would love to upgrade my MacBook Pro’s RAM from the stock 4GB to 16GB and ditch the optical drive to get a SSD for the OS and software. Also, a huge display, as big as my computer can handle.
I’m starting to feel the need to upgrade my iPod touch, too.
Other than that, I’m fine, but if my whims were to be satisfied I want a Launchpad Pro and a bigger Scarlett interface. I would LOVE to have a Teenage Engineering OP-1 and an Arturia MicroBrute, too. Also, all the Korg Volcas.
But yeah, I’m doing fine right now. Oh, I remember one thing: I desperately need a pair of quality headphones.
